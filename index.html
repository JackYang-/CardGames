Rules:

The first player who is dealt the elusive 3 of diamonds gets the first turn

A player during their turn may play any of the viable card combinations listed below if no cards have been played at all. If someone else has already played some cards, then the player may only play card combinations that trumps the last play. If the player does not wish to play, then they can pass their turn. The trump rules and conditions are as follows.

Singles

How to play: any one card of any suit.

How to beat: play another single that's strictly bigger. The order of cards from smallest to biggest is 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, jack, queen, king, ace, 2, black joker, red joker.

Doubles

How to play: two cards with the same number of any suit.

How to beat: play another double that's strictly bigger.

Triples

How to play: three cards with the same number of any suit plus zero, one or two of any card.

How to beat: play another triple that's strictly bigger with any number of extra cards.

Straights

How to play: a chain of singles, doubles, or triples with consecutive numbers. Single straights must be at least 5 cards long. Double straights and triple straights must be at least 2 doubles/triples long. Triple straights of length L can be played with up to any 2L cards. A straight may start at 3 and may end at up to Aces. Two's and jokers cannot be used as a part of a straight unless they were extra cards for a triple straight.

How to beat: play another straight of the same type that starts at a higher value.

Five Ten King

Name sake of the game! Five Ten Kings automatically trump ALL of the plays listed above.

How to play: a single 5, a single 10, and a single king with any suit.

How to beat: If the 3 Five Ten King cards are not of the same suit, then another Five Ten King with all 3 cards of the same suit will beat it. Same suit Five Ten Kings cannot trump eachother. Any Five Ten King play will be beaten by Quads (see below).

Quads

The most powerful play available. Quads trump every possible play except themselves.

How to play: four cards of the same number.

How to beat: play a bigger quad
